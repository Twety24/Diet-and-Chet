#Our Ingredients For Stuffed Grape Leaves
Fast Good Stuffed Grape Leaves
1 cup brown rice or quinoa, cooked
1 onion, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 medium tomato, finely chopped
1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
Salt to taste
Fresh grape leaves, rinsed and drained
#Our Steps For Stuffed Grape Leaves
Here Are How To Do
Prepare the Filling: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked brown rice or quinoa, chopped onion, minced garlic, chopped tomato, parsley, mint, pepper, cinnamon, allspice, and salt. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed. Stuff the Grape Leaves:
Place a grape leaf on a flat surface, with the shiny side facing down and the stem end toward you. Add a small amount of the filling (about 1 tablespoon) near the stem end of the leaf. Fold the sides of the leaf over the filling, then roll it up tightly from the stem end to the tip. Repeat with the remaining leaves and filling. Assemble and Cook:
Line the bottom of a large pot with a few unstuffed grape leaves to prevent sticking. Arrange the stuffed grape leaves in the pot, seam side down, in a single layer. If necessary, create additional layers, but make sure to pack them tightly. Place lemon slices on top of the stuffed grape leaves. Add enough water to the pot to just cover the grape leaves. Place a heatproof plate on top to keep the leaves from unraveling during cooking. Cover the pot and bring the water to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 45-60 minutes, or until the grape leaves are tender and the filling is fully cooked. Serve:
Let the stuffed grape leaves cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.
